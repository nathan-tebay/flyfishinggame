DECISIONS LOG

[1] INSECTS MOVED TO WORLD SPACE
Problem: InsectParticle nodes were children of InsectLayer (CanvasLayer), putting them in screen coordinates. They scrolled with the camera instead of drifting with the river.
Decision: Spawn InsectParticles as direct children of RiverWorld (Node2D), set z_index=5. Downstream velocity = positive x: randf_range(40.0, 90.0) px/s. Wrap range is world-space x centred on angler ±1 viewport width.
Rationale: World-space children move independently of the camera. The insect-as-entity model (world velocity + world wrap) is physically correct and required for future insect-to-fly-line distance checks.
Files: river_world.gd (_spawn_insects rewrite, _insect_nodes array), insect_particle.gd (comment fix, net-downstream skitter bias).

[2] FAR BANK ACCESS — TILE-BASED WADING LIMIT
Problem: _max_wade_y() used a fixed distance cap from the near bank. Any deep section blocked progression before the far bank; weed beds and gravel bars mid-river were also treated as impassable barriers.
Decision: _max_wade_y() scans tile-by-tile from the first water row downward. Stops at the last passable row above TILE_DEEP (dark blue = impassable). At TILE_RIVERBED without any prior TILE_DEEP = ford/shallow crossing → returns far bank centre y. At TILE_BANK = lands on far bank.
Rationale: The tile type is the ground truth for passability. TILE_DEEP is dark blue water that is genuinely too deep to wade. Everything else in the water column (surface, mid, riverbed, weed, gravel, rock) is passable. The tile-scan correctly handles every case without special rules.
Files: angler.gd (_max_wade_y, _near_bank_edge_y).

[3] BOTTOM_BANK_PROFILE — FAR BANK TRACKING
Problem: No per-column record of where the far bank starts, making it impossible to reliably detect ford vs. deep section or snap the angler to the far bank surface.
Decision: Added bottom_bank_profile[x] to RiverData. Set in _build_tile_map() as riverbed_row + 1 (first far-bank tile row). Used in _max_wade_y() for ford detection and in _snap_to_bank_surface() for far-bank snap target.
Rationale: Both near and far bank profiles must be known per-column to correctly handle the curved, variable-width river. Without this the far bank was effectively a mystery — you could enter it but not land cleanly.
Files: river_data.gd (new field), river_generator.gd (_init_arrays, _build_tile_map), angler.gd (_max_wade_y, _snap_to_bank_surface, _sync_wading_state).

[4] FAR BANK RETURN MOVEMENT
Problem: After landing on the far bank and pressing UP, the angler was blocked — the existing UP handler was capped by the near bank edge, making it impossible to re-enter the water from the far side.
Decision: Three-branch UP logic:
  - Wading: cap movement upward at near bank edge (_near_bank_edge_y).
  - Far bank (position.y > near_edge_y + 2 tiles): move freely upward to re-enter water.
  - Near bank: no action needed (snap handles surface tracking).
Rationale: Re-entering water from the far bank is the reverse of entering from the near bank. The two-tile margin between "near bank edge" and "far bank territory" is enough to distinguish the cases without overlap.
Files: angler.gd (_handle_movement vertical branch).

[5] HORIZONTAL Y-CLAMP ON MOVEMENT
Problem: Angler could wade deep in a pool column then walk horizontally into a ford column at that same y, ending up in TILE_AIR below the ford's far bank. _sync_wading_state would see TILE_AIR → is_wading=false, _snap_to_bank_surface threshold was too small to recover.
Decision: After any horizontal movement, clamp position.y = minf(position.y, _max_wade_y()). This pulls the angler up to the new column's wading limit any time they walk from deep to shallow.
Rationale: Horizontal movement can change the max wadable y by many tiles if the column changes drastically (deep pool → ford). Clamping after every move prevents drifting into invalid tile states.
Files: angler.gd (_handle_movement horizontal section).

[6] CURVED BANKS — WIDER VARIATION
Problem: Near bank noise frequency 0.003 gave only 3–5 tile variation (64px), too subtle to notice while playing. Both banks needed independent curvature with dramatic wide/narrow sections.
Decision:
  - Near bank: frequency 0.0018 (~555 tile wavelength), range BANK_H_TILES to BANK_H_TILES+4 (3–7 tiles).
  - Far bank: independent noise, seed+33333, frequency 0.0035, range 3–5 tiles.
  - Per-column water cap: mini(max_depth, data.height - top_bank_h - bot_bank_h - 1) prevents riverbed overflow.
Rationale: Long wavelength (0.0018) creates broad meander-like curves visible over hundreds of tiles. Independent seeds ensure the two banks don't mirror each other. The per-column cap prevents tile overflow in columns where both banks are at maximum height.
Files: river_generator.gd (_build_tile_map).

[7] FORD INJECTION — GUARANTEED CROSSINGS
Problem: Default depth profile generates depth_val >= 0.20, which always produces TILE_DEEP (threshold at depth_val ≈ 0.345). Without fords, no section was crossable.
Decision:
  - Ford centre depth: FORD_DEPTH = 0.06 (guaranteed below 0.345 → no TILE_DEEP generated).
  - Ford width: FORD_WIDTH = 60 tiles (wider = easier to find).
  - Ford spacing: FORD_SPACING = 320 tiles → 4–5 fords per 1440-tile section.
  - Guaranteed fallback: if ford_xs is empty, one ford at section midpoint.
  - Profile injection: cosine taper from current depth at edges to FORD_DEPTH at centre.
Rationale: Fords are the only places players can cross. Without guaranteed fords the game is unplayable for far-bank fishing. The cosine taper avoids cliff-like transitions in the depth profile.
Files: river_generator.gd (_inject_ford_sections).

[8] UNDERCUT BANK PLACEMENT FIX
Problem: Undercut bank structures used y == BANK_H_TILES (constant 3) as the valid placement row. With top_bank_h varying 3–7, undercut banks could be placed inside solid bank tiles on wide-bank columns.
Decision: _valid_placement() check changed to y == data.top_bank_profile[x] — the actual first water row for the column.
Rationale: The undercut bank tile must sit at the water-bank boundary. That row is per-column, not constant, after the curved bank changes.
Files: river_generator.gd (_valid_placement).

[9] WADING DEPTH INDICATOR — COLUMN-RELATIVE SCALING
Problem: The blue depth bar used MAX_WADE_DEPTH * TILE_SIZE (320px) as the denominator. A ford with only 4 water tiles (128px) would show max 0.40 at the far bank. A pool blocked at TILE_DEEP at row 7 (160px total reach from surface) would show 0.25 max. The bar rarely reached meaningful values.
Decision: denominator = (bottom_bank_profile[col] - top_bank_profile[col]) * TILE_SIZE — the actual water column height for the current column. Result: deep pools max at ~0.4 because TILE_DEEP blocks at 40% depth. Fords reach 1.0 at the far bank. Shallower columns show proportionally lower readings.
Rationale: The depth reading should reflect position within the wadable range of the current column, not a fixed global scale. A reading of 0.8 means "very deep" for whatever column you're in, not "80% of MAX_WADE_DEPTH".
Files: angler.gd (_sync_wading_state).

BUG FIXES

[B1] Angler stopped mid-river on horizontal movement (TILE_AIR drift)
  Files: angler.gd
  Problem: Walking from a deep column to a ford at deep-pool y caused angler to land in TILE_AIR.
  is_wading=false, far-bank snap threshold (1 tile) was too small to recover from mid-river TILE_AIR.
  Fix: Clamp position.y after horizontal move. Increased snap threshold to 6 tiles.

[B2] Far bank UP movement blocked
  Files: angler.gd
  Problem: UP key was gated on `is_wading`, so standing on the dry far bank and pressing UP did nothing.
  Fix: Third movement branch for far bank: `elif position.y > ne_y + TILE_SIZE * 2.0: position.y -= WADE_SPEED_V * delta`.

[B3] TILE_DEEP at water_start → angler enters water
  Files: angler.gd
  Problem: When TILE_DEEP started immediately at the first water row (very deep near-bank section), _max_wade_y() would return TILE_DEEP row centre, allowing the angler to enter. The tile scan now returns _near_bank_edge_y() if TILE_DEEP is the first row encountered — can't enter at all.

TECHNICAL NOTES

- TILE_DEEP threshold: lerpf(-0.2, 0.38, depth_val). At depth_val=0.06 (ford): threshold=-0.168 — centre_d (always 0..1) never negative → no TILE_DEEP generated. Guaranteed crossable fords.
- Current map still uses MAX_DEPTH_TILES as the scale denominator (slightly imprecise for per-column-capped sections), but functionally correct — ford speed is always high (narrow channel), deep pool speed is always low.
- _near_bank_edge_y() returns (top_bank_profile[col] - 1) * TILE_SIZE + TILE_SIZE * 0.5 — the last bank tile's centre. This is the snap target and the UP key cap.
- snap_to_bank_surface far-bank threshold: 6.0 tiles to handle mid-river recovery from TILE_AIR landing.
