DECISIONS LOG

[1] MINIMUM RIVER WIDTH — MIN_DEPTH_TILES 4 → 14
Problem: Random depth profiles could produce very narrow river cross-sections, sometimes as few as 4 tiles wide. These were unplayable and visually wrong.
Decision: Changed MIN_DEPTH_TILES in river_constants.gd from 4 to 14. River is always at least 14 tiles (~112 px) wide at its narrowest.
Rationale: The Madison River is a wide freestone river. Narrow choke points read as irrigation ditches, not trout water.
Files: river_constants.gd.

[2] BOULDER AND ROCK WAKE SEAM LINES
Problem: Boulder wake zones (dead water, side eddies, V-wake) were rendered as depth-map darkening only. The current seam lines — where fast water meets the slower wake — had no visual indicator.
Decision: Added `_add_wake_seams()` helper called from `_build_rock_clusters`. Draws Line2D pairs along the wake edges:
  - Teardrop shape: half-width = max_half × (1 - t²) — quadratic convergence to a point downstream.
  - Confined to water column: line points clamped to top_bank_profile / bottom_bank_profile px bounds.
  - Applied to both boulders (wake_tiles=9, max_half=rw×1.10) and regular rocks (wake_tiles=5, max_half=rw×0.90).
  - Alpha gradient: 0.55 → 0.0 from rock to tip; colour a pale blue-white (Color(0.55, 0.80, 0.95)).
  - Antialiased Line2D, width 1.2 px, z_index=1.
Rationale: Current seams are where dry flies drag and where fish hold. Making them visible trains the player to read the river. The teardrop convergence matches real wake geometry — the two seam lines meet at the downstream tip of the turbulence zone.
Files: river_renderer.gd (_add_wake_seams, _build_rock_clusters).

[3] LOADING SCREEN
Problem: River generation is blocking — the game froze for several frames before the first render on section load. No feedback to the player.
Decision: Added `scripts/ui/loading_screen.gd` — a Node2D with _draw() painting a full-screen dark overlay (Color(0.05, 0.09, 0.16)) with two text lines:
  "GENERATING RIVER..." (large, centred)
  "reading the water" (smaller subtitle)
In river_world.gd: added _LoadingScreenScript preload, _loading_canvas: CanvasLayer, _show_loading() / _hide_loading() methods. Made _ready() async — show loading → await process_frame × 2 → generate → hide loading. The double frame await ensures the loading screen paints before the blocking generation call.
Files: scripts/ui/loading_screen.gd (new), scripts/river/river_world.gd.

[4] ISLANDS
Problem: River had no mid-channel features. Real rivers, especially wide freestone rivers, have gravel islands — permanent or seasonal — that break up current and create holding seams on both sides.
Decision: Added `_place_islands()` step in RiverGenerator, called after _build_tile_map and before _generate_current_map. Each island:
  - Length: 10–22 tiles.
  - Shape: sine-tapered — half_h = int(round(MAX_HALF_H × sin(frac × PI))) — so it tapers to a point at both ends.
  - Spacing: 120–320 tiles apart (seeded random interval).
  - Tiles written as TILE_BANK into the river columns.
  - Placement: centred vertically between top and bottom bank profiles; rejected if it would reach either bank.
Rationale: Islands create current seams on both flanks — prime feeding lanes for trout. They also add visual variety and navigation challenge for wading.
Files: river_generator.gd (_place_islands).

[5] DRIFTWOOD LOGS IN RIVER
Problem: River had no large woody debris. Fallen logs are common in western rivers and are prime cover structure (shade, shelter, bark invertebrate habitat).
Decision: Added TILE_LOG (id=10) to RiverConstants:
  - Color: Color(0.38, 0.24, 0.12) — dark bark brown.
  - STRUCTURE_COVER[10] = 0.8, STRUCTURE_HATCH[10] = 0.5.
  - _DEPTH_RANK_F[TILE_LOG] = 2.6 in renderer (shallow-ish, sits at water surface).
In river_generator.gd: TILE_LOG placed by `_place_structures` at `int(7 × density)` count per section. Placement restricted to bank-edge water tiles (y ≤ near_edge+2 or y ≥ far_start-3). Structure width: randi_range(4,9) tiles; height: 1 tile.
In river_renderer.gd: `_build_log_nodes()` called from render(). Three driftwood variants:
  - Straight log (40%): tapered rectangle, _draw_driftwood_straight.
  - Tapered log (35%): asymmetric taper, _draw_driftwood_tapered.
  - Forked log (25%): Y-shaped split, _draw_driftwood_forked.
  Angle categories: ±14° (30%), 22–52° (30%), 62–86° near-perpendicular (40%).
  Colour: weathered grey palette via _bark_colors(rng) — greens 0.38–0.72, desaturated.
  Dead branch stubs: _add_branch_stubs() adds 2–5 short Line2D branches per log (snapped off, weathered grey).
Rationale: Logs that have been in the river for years look grey and weathered with broken branch stubs. The near-perpendicular bias matches how logs tend to come to rest — carried sideways by floods and deposited across current. They are some of the most reliable fish cover in western rivers.
Files: river_constants.gd, river_generator.gd, river_renderer.gd (_build_log_nodes, _draw_driftwood_straight, _draw_driftwood_tapered, _draw_driftwood_forked, _add_branch_stubs, _bark_colors).

[6] BANK VEGETATION AND ROCKS
Problem: Bank tiles were visually empty — flat green with no texture or vegetation. The near and far banks had nothing to suggest riparian habitat.
Decision: Added `_build_bank_features()` to river_renderer.gd, called from render(). Three feature types:
  - Trees: larger canopy (crown_r: ts×0.75–1.30), textured (branch spokes, inner shadow, highlight, bark streaks), lean variation.
  - Bushes: texture-patch approach — 5–9 overlapping small ellipses per tile, varied green hues and alpha, no distinct silhouette.
  - Boulders/rocks: texture-patch approach — 3–5 small gray blobs per tile, varied grey tones, no facets or cracks.
Coverage (final tuning after several iterations):
  - Every column sampled (no stride), every bank tile eligible.
  - Trees: 6.25% exclusive (no other feature on tree tile).
  - Bushes: ~89% of remaining tiles (r < 0.95, continuous ground cover).
  - Boulders: 30% independent roll (can co-occur with bushes).
  - 1 row clearance from water edge on both banks (near: ty < near_edge-1; far: ty > far_start).
  - Far-bank and near-bank conditions checked independently (bug: flat `ty >= near_edge-1` was blocking all far-bank rows).
Patch placement: each patch centre randomised within tile — `rng.randf_range(ts×0.1, ts×0.9)` on both x and y axes, so cover is not grid-aligned.
Sizes (after tuning): bush spread ts×0.57, blob radius ts×0.135–0.30; boulder spread ts×0.42, blob radius ts×0.105–0.24.
Rationale: Riparian vegetation is dense and continuous on a western river. Bushes (willows, alders) grow right to the water's edge and are the dominant visual element on the bank. Rocks and boulders are scattered throughout. The texture-patch approach avoids individual-object recognition — the bank reads as ground cover, not a collection of placed props.
Files: river_renderer.gd (_build_bank_features, _draw_bank_tree, _draw_bank_bush, _draw_bank_boulder).

[7] ANGLER Z-INDEX FIX
Problem: Angler sprite was rendering behind bank features (bushes, boulders) placed by _build_bank_features. The player character was occluded by vegetation drawn on lower z-index children.
Decision: Added `z_index = 5` in Angler._ready(). Renderer bank feature overlays use z_index 1–2. Angler at 5 renders above all of them.
Rationale: The player character must always be visible. Vegetation occlusion is visually plausible in real life but creates a readability problem — the player loses their character in dense brush.
Files: scripts/angler/angler.gd.

BUGS ENCOUNTERED AND RESOLVED

[B1] Far-bank features not rendering
Root cause: `if ty >= near_edge - 1: continue` in _build_bank_features. For far-bank rows, ty is large (e.g. 25+) and near_edge is ~3, so the condition always triggered. All far-bank tiles were skipped.
Fix: Split the clearance check by bank side:
  if ty < far_start:
      if ty >= near_edge - 1: continue   # near bank
  else:
      if ty == far_start: continue       # far bank
Files: river_renderer.gd (_build_bank_features).

[B2] 2-row clearance killing near-bank coverage
Earlier iteration used `ty >= near_edge - 2` (2-row clearance). With near_edge=3, this leaves only ty=0. Combined with STRIDE=2, almost nothing rendered. Reverted to 1-row clearance.
Files: river_renderer.gd (_build_bank_features).

[B3] FastNoiseLite cluster gate too aggressive
Cluster noise gate (< 0.22 skip) caused sparse/absent coverage when combined with the low bank tile count (3 rows near bank). Removed gate for final ground-cover approach — continuous coverage better serves the visual goal than patchy clusters.
Files: river_renderer.gd (_build_bank_features).
